Background

Why Message-Passing?

  • Classical ML: Nodes as independent samples (tabular).
  • Message-Passing: Leverages topological structure.
  • Captures dependencies between neighboring nodes.
  • Superior performance in Node Classification by aggregating local neighborhoods.

The Neighborhood Explosion

  • L-layer GNN aggregates L-hop neighbors.
  • Average degree $d$ leads to $d^L$ nodes.
  • Exponential growth makes full-batch training impossible on large graphs.

Exponential expansion per hop

Sampling Strategies

  • GraphSAINT: Subgraph sampling to decouple GNN depth from neighborhood size.
  • GraphSAGE: Neighborhood sampling (fixed-size).
    • Uniformly samples $k$ neighbors per hop.
    • Allows mini-batch training on massive graphs.

PyTorch Geometric (PyG)

  • Industry standard for GNNs.
  • RAM-based: Loads entire graph into memory.
  • Excellent GPU utilization.
  • The Cost: High memory requirement; scaling to billions of edges is prohibitively expensive.

Neo4j: The Graph Platform

  • Tabular vs Graph: Relationships are first-class citizens.
  • Cypher: Declarative graph query language.
  • UDP (User Defined Procedures): Custom Java-based extensions for high-performance traversal.
